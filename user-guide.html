1. Logging In & Authentication

Secure access to your tenant workspace

How to log in

  1. Open the application

    Navigate to the ERP URL provided by your administrator (e.g., http://localhost or your hosted domain).

  2. Enter your credentials

    Type your email address and password then click Sign In.

  3. You're in

    You land on the Dashboard. Your name and role appear in the top-right corner. A JWT access token is silently refreshed every session.

Forgotten password? Contact your Super Admin or Admin. They can reset your account from the Users page.
Session security: Tokens expire automatically. If you are idle for an extended period you will be redirected to the login screen — simply sign in again.

3. Navigation & Common UI Patterns

How to use the sidebar, tables, modals, and pagination

Sidebar navigation

The left sidebar groups all modules. Collapsed on small screens (hamburger menu). Your current page is highlighted in indigo.

Search bars

Every list page has a search field at the top-left. Type to filter results instantly — results refresh as you type (no need to press Enter).

Pagination

Long lists are paginated (default 20 rows). Use the ← / → buttons at the bottom of the table to navigate between pages.

Modals (pop-up forms)

Creating or editing records opens a modal overlay. Required fields are marked with *. Press Cancel or click outside the modal to close without saving.

Status badges

Coloured pills indicate record status throughout the app:

ACTIVE / PAID / APPROVED PENDING / DRAFT / PENDING_APPROVAL INACTIVE / OVERDUE / LOST CONFIRMED / CONVERTED NEW / OPEN CANCELLED / CLOSED

4. POS — Point of Sale / Caisse

Fast cashier interface for walk-in and counter sales

All roles with sales:CREATE

The POS screen is split into two panels: the Product Grid on the left and the Cart & Checkout on the right. It is designed for speed — no page reloads, no navigation.

Screen layout

Left panel — Products

  • Session bar: today's sale count & revenue
  • Search box to filter products by name or SKU
  • Product tiles showing name, SKU, sale price and type badge
  • Refresh button (↺) to sync session totals

Right panel — Cart

  • Item count badge
  • Line items with +/− quantity controls & remove (🗑)
  • Subtotal & Total
  • Payment method selector
  • Amount tendered + automatic change calculation
  • Charge button

Making a sale — step by step

  1. Search or browse products

    Type in the search box or scroll the grid. Each tile shows the product name, SKU, and sale price.

  2. Add items to cart

    Click any product tile to add it. Click the same tile again to increase the quantity by 1.

  3. Adjust quantities

    Use the − and + buttons next to each cart item. Pressing − when quantity is 1 removes the item.

  4. Select payment method

    Choose: Cash, Card, Mobile Money, or Bank Transfer.

  5. Enter amount tendered (optional for cash)

    Type the amount the customer hands you. The change due appears in green automatically.

  6. Click "Charge [Total]"

    The system creates the sale order, records the payment, and shows the receipt modal.

  7. Print or close receipt

    Click Print Receipt to send to printer, or close the modal. The cart clears automatically.

Walk-in customers: The POS creates sales as "Walk-in" by default. No customer record is required.
Session totals in the top bar refresh automatically after each checkout. Click the ↺ icon to force a refresh.

Payment methods

MethodUse case
CASHPhysical currency — change is calculated
CARDDebit/credit card terminal
MOBILE_MONEYMobile payment (M-Pesa, Wave, etc.)
BANK_TRANSFERDirect bank-to-bank

Stock movements

Every time inventory changes (purchase received, sale, adjustment, return) a movement record is created automatically. You can also record manual adjustments.

Movement typeEffect on stockTriggered by
INIncreases quantityPurchase received, manual stock-in
OUTDecreases quantitySale confirmed, manual stock-out

Low stock alerts

When a product's stock level drops below its Min Stock threshold, it appears on the Dashboard under "Low Stock Count" and in the Analytics inventory summary. Set minStock when creating or editing a product.

SKU uniqueness: Two products cannot share the same SKU within your tenant. The system returns an error on duplicate SKU.

NEW → CONTACTED → QUALIFIED → CONVERTED  or  LOST

Creating a lead

PhoneOptionalContact phone number
CompanyOptionalCompany or organisation name

Converting a lead to a customer

  1. Find the lead

    Use the search bar or scroll the leads table to find the lead you want to convert.

  2. Click "Convert"

    The orange "Convert" button appears in the Actions column for leads that are not yet CONVERTED or LOST.

Managing permission overrides

Beyond role-based permissions, you can grant individual permissions directly to a user. These are additive — the user keeps their role permissions plus these extras.

  1. Click "Permissions" on the user's row

    The shield + arrow button opens the Permission Overrides modal.

  2. Review existing overrides

    Overrides are grouped by module (e.g., INVENTORY: CREATE UPDATE). Remove any with the ✕ button.

  3. Add a new override

    Click Add Permission Override, select a permission from the dropdown (e.g., finance:APPROVE), and click Grant.

When to use overrides: Use them sparingly for exceptions — e.g., a Sales Manager who also needs read access to Finance. Prefer assigning a second role instead when the need is permanent.

Managing roles & permissions

Super Admin Admin

Viewing a role's permissions

Click any role in the left list. The right panel shows the permission matrix: rows = modules, columns = actions (READ, CREATE, UPDATE, DELETE, APPROVE). Filled coloured buttons = permission granted.

Creating a custom role

  1. Click "New" button

    Top-right of the role list (visible to Admins only).

  2. Enter name and optional description

    Click Create.

  3. Assign permissions

    Select the new role in the list. The matrix is empty. Click individual cells or click a module name to toggle all actions for that module. Click Save changes.

Cloning a role

Click the Clone button on any role card. A copy is created with the same permissions as a new custom role named "Original Name (Copy)". Useful for creating role variants.

Enabling / Disabling a role

Click the orange Disable or green Enable button on a role card. Disabled roles:

  • Are shown with a Disabled badge
  • Cannot be assigned to new users
  • Users already holding the role lose the permissions it granted until it is re-enabled

Editing a role

Click the Edit (pencil) button to rename the role or update its description. Not available for the Super Admin role.

Deleting a role

Click the red Delete button. Only custom (non-system) roles can be deleted. System roles (Super Admin, Admin, HR Manager, etc.) are protected.

Warning: Deleting a custom role removes it from all users assigned to it. Verify no active users depend on the role before deleting.

Editing system role permissions

System roles (lock icon) can only have their permissions modified by an Admin or Super Admin. Lower roles see a read-only view with a "Only the Admin can modify system roles" notice.

19. Tips & Best Practices

Getting the most out of the ERP platform

Daily operations checklist

  1. Check the Dashboard first

    Review the Low Stock count and Outstanding balance before starting your day.

  2. Process POS transactions in real time

    Do not batch POS entries — enter each sale as it happens for accurate session totals and live inventory.

  3. Confirm purchase orders promptly

    Move POs from DRAFT to CONFIRMED once sent to the supplier, and to RECEIVED when goods arrive to trigger inventory updates.

  4. Review customer aging weekly

    Run the Customer Aging report every week to follow up on OVERDUE invoices before they become bad debt.

  5. Approve budget plans before the fiscal period starts

    Plans must be APPROVED to show in the Dashboard active count and to be used for variance analysis.

Data hygiene tips

Unique codes matter. Customer codes, supplier codes, and product SKUs must be unique. Develop a consistent naming convention from the start (e.g., CUST-001, SUPP-001, PRD-001).
Set min stock thresholds. Enter a minStock value for every physical product. This powers the Low Stock alert on the Dashboard and Analytics page.
Keep roles lean. Prefer assigning one well-defined role per user. Use direct permission overrides only for genuine exceptions, not as a way to avoid creating a proper role.
Deactivate, don't delete. When an employee leaves, disable their user account (change status to INACTIVE) rather than deleting it. Historical records linked to that user (sales, approvals) remain intact.
Currency consistency. Set your base currency in Settings → Company before entering any financial records. Changing it later does not retroactively convert stored amounts.
